# Resilience Defined: Homepage Structure Plan

This document outlines the strategic homepage structure for the Resilience Defined website, applying UX patterns to create a strong, calm, and athletic user experience focused on joint longevity and science-backed training.

## 1. Hero Section (High Impact)
*   **Purpose:** Make an immediate, powerful first impression. Establish the brand identity (strong but calm athletic feel) and immediately communicate the value proposition of joint longevity and resilience.
*   **Headline Direction Ideas:**
    *   "Build a Body That Lasts."
    *   "Strength Built on Resilience."
    *   "Pain-Free Movement. Unbreakable Joints."
*   **Suggested CTA Actions:** Primary: "Start Your Training" (Solid Gold/Red Button) | Secondary: "Explore Our Methodology" (Ghost Button/Outline)
*   **Notes:** This section must feel absolutely **premium and cinematic**. Use a full-width dark background (video or high-quality calm athletic imagery), stark white bold typography, and a prominent accent color (red or gold) for the primary CTA to drive immediate conversion intent.

## 2. Social Proof & Authority (Trust Building)
*   **Purpose:** Quickly establish trust and authority before asking the user to commit to a program. Validate the science-backed approach.
*   **Headline Direction Ideas:**
    *   "Trusted by Athletes. Backed by Science."
    *   "Real Resilience. Proven Results."
*   **Suggested CTA Actions:** "Read Success Stories" (Subtle text link)
*   **Notes:** Keep this section clean and minimal. Use logos of featured publications/collaborators, or a highly curated slider of 3-4 impactful text quotes focusing on the transition from pain to mobility. This section transitions from the premium hero into the educational body of the site.

## 3. Core Training Programs (Grid Layout)
*   **Purpose:** Present the primary offerings (Longevity Training, Strength, etc.) in a digestible, easily scannable format allowing users to self-select their path.
*   **Headline Direction Ideas:**
    *   "Choose Your Path to Resilience."
    *   "Science-Backed Programs for Every Body."
*   **Suggested CTA Actions:** "View Program Details" or "Start [Program Name]" on each card.
*   **Notes:** This is a **highly conversion-focused** section. Use a 3-column dark-mode card grid. Each card needs an athletic action shot, a bold white title, and pill-shaped tags (e.g., `Joint Health`, `Strength`, `Mobility`). The layout should feel structured and modern with subtle hover state animations (e.g., card slightly lifts or border glows gold).

## 4. The Methodology (Split Feature Layout)
*   **Purpose:** Explain *how* and *why* Resilience Defined is different. Emphasize the science-backed, long-term approach to tendon strength and joint longevity.
*   **Headline Direction Ideas:**
    *   "The Science of Staying Strong."
    *   "We Don't Just Build Muscle. We Build Resilience."
*   **Suggested CTA Actions:** "Learn About Our Science" or "Read the Manifesto"
*   **Notes:** Use a 50/50 split layout. One half should feature a strong, detailed image (perhaps anatomical overlay on an athlete or a calm training environment), and the other half should contain clean, legible descriptive text. High visual hierarchy is needed here: large headers and readable paragraphs.

## 5. Joint Health Library (Educational Feature)
*   **Purpose:** Highlight the specific, targeted resources available for individual joint issues (knees, shoulders, lower back), positioning the brand as an educational authority.
*   **Headline Direction Ideas:**
    *   "Target Your Weak Links."
    *   "The Joint Health Library."
*   **Suggested CTA Actions:** "Explore the Library" or "Find Your Fix"
*   **Notes:** Use a sleek, horizontal scroll or a staggered grid layout. Use iconography or focused photography for specific body parts. This section should feel highly organized and accessible, driving engagement into the free/premium educational content.

## 6. Free Resource / Lead Capture (Sticky or Full-Width Banner)
*   **Purpose:** Capture emails by offering actionable, high-value free advice.
*   **Headline Direction Ideas:**
    *   "Start Your Recovery Today. Get the Free Mobility Guide."
    *   "Weekly Joint Health Strategies to Your Inbox."
*   **Suggested CTA Actions:** "Download Free Guide" or "Subscribe"
*   **Notes:** **Conversion-focused (Lead Gen).** Use a dedicated block with a splash of brand red to break up the dark theme and draw the eye. Keep the input form simple (just email, or name + email).

## 7. Footer (Expansive Utility)
*   **Purpose:** Provide comprehensive navigation, legal links, social connections, and a final touchpoint for the brand.
*   **Headline Direction Ideas:** N/A (Standard footer titles: Explore, Resources, Legal)
*   **Suggested CTA Actions:** Secondary newsletter signup, social media icons.
*   **Notes:** Black background, grey text, clean columns. Ensures the user always has a clear path forward even if they scroll to the very bottom without clicking a primary CTA.
